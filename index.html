Welcome to the TheoChemMerida Research Group!

The TheoChemMerida Research Group is part of the Applied Physics Department at the Centro de Investigación y de Estudios Avanzados (CINVESTAV), Unidad Mérida. The group is led by Dr. Gabriel Merino and Dr. Filiberto Ortiz Chi, and focuses on the use of modern computational chemistry tools to understand and predict the properties of novel organic and inorganic molecular systems.

Our research interests include planar hypercoordinate carbon molecules, systems containing multiple bonds, endohedral complexes, dimetallocenes, complex materials, and molecules under extreme conditions such as high pressure. A central objective of our work is to gain fundamental insight into the structure, bonding, and electron delocalization of these systems.

We are always looking for motivated and enthusiastic researchers to join our group. If you are interested in graduate or postdoctoral studies in theoretical or experimental chemistry, please feel free to contact us for further information.

Research Areas

Aromaticity and Chemical Bonding

Study of aromaticity, electron delocalization, and unconventional bonding patterns in molecular systems.

Computational & Theoretical Chemistry

Quantum chemical modeling of molecular structure, stability, and reactivity using advanced theoretical methods.

Reaction Mechanisms & Boron Chemistry

Investigation of chemical reaction pathways, boron-containing compounds, and activation of small molecules.

Automated Workflows & Data-Driven Chemistry

Development of automated computational workflows and data-assisted approaches for chemical research.

Materials & Molecular Systems

Theoretical study of molecular materials, organometallic complexes, and functional molecular systems.

Chemistry Under Extreme Conditions

Exploration of molecular behavior under high pressure and other extreme physical conditions.
